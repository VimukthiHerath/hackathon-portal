how to submit

Five steps, no forms outside GitHub

You'll need a free GitHub account. Everything else happens on this site and in one GitHub Issue.

  1. Download the starter pack

    On the Questions page, find the question you're solving and download its starter pack. The problem statement itself lives on that page too — read it there before you start.

  2. Solve it locally

    Write your solution in the starter file. Use the sample input/output in the problem statement to check your work before you submit.

  3. Zip your solution file

    Compress just your solution file into a standard .zip. No project folders or extra files needed.

  4. Open a submission issue

    Click the button below, select the right question from the dropdown, and drag your .zip into the upload field. Submitting the issue is the same as submitting your solution.

    Open submission issue

  5. Track your result

    Watch the issue you opened — a comment will land on it confirming pass or fail, along with your score. The issue closes automatically once that comment is posted, and your score appears on the leaderboard right after.

Need to try again? Open a new submission issue for the same question with your updated zip. Your best result for each question is the one that counts.
